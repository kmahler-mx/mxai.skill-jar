## Ideal Client Profile

**Best-fit criteria (more important than industry or size):**
- Complex sales and buyer ecosystem
- Clear need to create awareness and demand in the marketplace
- Marketing budget of $300,000–$3,000,000/year (excluding media)
- Internal marketing infrastructure that supports program facilitation and stakeholder organization on the client side

**Target roles:**
- *Economic buyers:* CMO, VP of Marketing, occasionally CEO (at smaller companies)
- *Technical buyers:* CTO, CIO (when digital/technology is central)
- *Day-to-day contacts:* Director of Marketing, Director of Web & Digital Assets, Director of Content, Director of Demand Generation, and other function-specific marketing leaders

**Hiring triggers:**
1. New management team in place
2. Recent acquisitions requiring consolidation of messaging and GTM strategy
3. Change in financial ownership (e.g., PE firm acquisition)
4. New product or service requiring a differentiated go-to-market approach
5. Need for a new website or web application — current presence is nonexistent, outdated, or underperforming

**Bad-fit signals:**
- Lack of client stakeholder time or commitment
- Ill-defined budget or unwillingness to commit to a budget
- A deservedly poor reputation in their marketplace

**Geography:** Primarily United States. MX maintains global agency partnerships to serve global clients and has a small number of active global clients.

## Common Client Challenges

**Problems clients bring:**
- Lack of brand clarity — unclear or inconsistent positioning in the market
- Insufficient or unreliable revenue pipeline
- Frustration with previous agencies that didn't deliver
- Poor website or weak digital experience
- Sense that they are losing opportunities because their digital presence isn't strong enough

**What they've tried before:**
- Standard tactics done perfunctorily — tradeshows, print/digital advertising — often without strategy or consistency

- Outside vendors or agencies without the right B2B expertise or capacity

**Cost of the problem:**
- Direct cost of failed programs and wasted spend
- Opportunity cost of deals lost due to weak brand, pipeline, or digital presence
- Reputational damage with buyers in the market

## MX Methodology & Process

**Engagement structure:**
1. Discovery
2. Strategy clarification
3. Development (personas, market maps, brand messaging, positioning, website, digital assets, etc.)
4. Iterative development cycles with client approval processes
5. Market launch (website go-live, ABM/demand program launch, brand rollout, etc.)
6. Measurement and results review

**Timeline:** 45–90 days for simpler or fast-track engagements; up to 12 months for complex rebrands or multi-brand consolidation projects.

**Onboarding/discovery:** MX invests deeply in understanding the client's industry, competitive environment, buyer ecosystem, positioning, and sales channels before beginning work. This immersion is frequently done on MX's own time — not billed to the client — and is a core part of what enables the depth of knowledge MX becomes known for with each client.

**Reporting cadence:**
- *Execution progress:* Project management formats — Gantt charts, deliverable milestones — reported monthly and in quarterly business reviews
- *Results:* Data and analytics reporting tied to program type — views, click-throughs, qualified leads, SEO/GEO/AEO performance, time on site, engagement rates, downloads, form fills, etc.

**What makes it distinctive:** MX's willingness to invest their own time in genuine deep understanding of the client's business before billing begins. This is the foundation of the "we know your business as well as you do" reputation and is not standard practice among competitors.
